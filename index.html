U.S. AirQuality 1985-2022

Historically, Pittsburgh's heavy industry brought both economic gains and environmental issues. From 1985 to 1995, the city saw worsening air quality, culminating in a peak AQI of 221. However, post-2005, stricter emission regulations from the revised Clean Air Act led to consistent air quality improvements. By 2020, good air quality days reached 218 with a significantly lower maximum AQI of 128. Pittsburgh's progress highlights the potential for environmental improvement through policy enforcement, technological advancement, and societal commitment.

Co2 Emission Of Transaportation In Pittsburgh

Our research focuses on the carbon emissions from transportation in Pittsburgh between 2018 and 2021. Despite improved air quality, carbon emissions from transportation remain a challenge. Traffic flow entering and leaving the city contributes more to carbon emissions than traffic flow within the city. We analyzed carbon emissions data at three locations: "inbound," "outbound," and "in boundary." By understanding the contribution of traffic flow at these locations to overall carbon emissions, we can develop more accurate carbon emission reduction strategies. From 2018 to 2021, carbon emissions at these locations have decreased, indicating positive effects from the deindustrialization and urbanization processes.

Emissions by Economic Sector, CO2 1990-2020

Pennsylvania's CO2 emissions across different sectors have changed from 1990 to 2020, reflecting the state's economic transformations. The industrial sector, which previously characterized Pittsburgh's economy, peaked in emissions in 2018 at 80.60 MMT CO2 equivalent, but by 2020, it had reduced to 76.88 MMT CO2 equivalent. However, the Electric Power Industry substantially decreased its emissions from 131.09 MMT CO2 equivalent in 2007 to 73.01 MMT CO2 equivalent in 2020. The commercial and residential sectors showed relatively stable emission trends. Overall, while deindustrialization led to a decrease in CO2 emissions, the transition to a service-based economy poses environmental challenges. Sustainable practices across all sectors are necessary for reducing overall emissions as Pittsburgh continues to evolve.
